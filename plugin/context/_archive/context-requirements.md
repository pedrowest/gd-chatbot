Core Musical Knowledge

Complete song catalog - all originals, covers, and variations with lyrics, composing credits, and evolution over time
Performance history - tour dates, venues, setlists, and notable shows from 1965-1995 (and beyond with derivative bands)
Discography - studio albums, official live releases (Dick's Picks, Dave's Picks, Road Trips, etc.), and archival release series
Musical evolution - how arrangements, tempos, and improvisational approaches changed across eras (60s psychedelia → 70s jazz-rock → 80s MIDI era)

Band Personnel & History

Member biographies - Jerry Garcia, Bob Weir, Phil Lesh, Bill Kreutzmann, Mickey Hart, Ron "Pigpen" McKernan, Keith Godchaux, Donna Jean Godchaux, Brent Mydland, Vince Welnick
Extended family - touring members, guest musicians, collaborators
Equipment and gear - Jerry's guitars (Tiger, Wolf), Phil's bass modifications, Dead-specific sound engineering innovations

Deadhead Culture & Scene

Taping culture - the band's permission for audience recording and its impact
Tour following culture - logistics, community, economics
Lot scene - vending, community gathering, pre-show culture
Regional scenes - geographic strongholds and community differences

Extended Universe

Derivative projects - Dead & Company, Furthur, Phil Lesh & Friends, RatDog, The Other Ones
Solo work - Jerry Garcia Band, Bob Weir's projects, etc.
Influences - bluegrass, folk, jazz, electronic music roots
Archive.org relationship - the Live Music Archive and taping permissions

Cultural Context

1960s counterculture - San Francisco scene, Acid Tests, Ken Kesey
Business model - unique approach to taping, merchandising, ticketing
Academic study - scholarly work on the Dead phenomenon
Media - documentaries (Long Strange Trip), books, journalism
