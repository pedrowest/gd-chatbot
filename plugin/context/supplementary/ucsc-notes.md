## History: The Band Chooses Santa Cruz

### April 24, 2008 - Official Donation Announcement

On April 24, 2008, band members Bob Weir and Mickey Hart announced at the San Francisco Fillmore press conference that the group was donating its archives to the University of California at Santa Cruz (UCSC) Library Special Collections.

**Key Points from the Press Conference:**
- Great excitement about making the archives available as a research collection and as a resource on the Internet
- The band's thirty-year history could be interpreted through educational use of archives and artifacts
- University Chancellor George Blumenthal displayed a tie-dyed T-shirt created to commemorate the occasion
- Bob Weir explained that the remaining members of the band agreed that making their collection available to the community was the right thing to do

### Why Santa Cruz?

Bob Weir: "We looked around, and UC Santa Cruz seems the best possible home. If you ever wrote the Grateful Dead a letter, you'll probably find it there!"

Mickey Hart called the decorated fan envelopes in the collection "masterpieces" showing the "love" between the band and the fans.

### Connection to UCSC

- Mickey Hart collaborated with the late UCSC Professor Fredric Lieberman on three books including Planet Drum
- Dr. Lieberman taught the course "Music of the Grateful Dead" where students discovered how the band re-envisioned and updated many of America's musical traditions, from bluegrass to jazz

### Eileen Law - Original Archivist

Eileen Law was originally hired to serve as a liaison to fans and became the band's original archivist. For over thirty-four years she saved everything.

- **Over 45,000 digitized items** from the UCSC Library's Grateful Dead Archive

  - EZID (persistent identifiers)

## Detailed Collection Information

### Dennis McNally Papers (MS 336), 1940-2010

**Extent**: 57 Linear Feet
- 35 half cartons
- 17 cartons
- 3 document boxes
- 6 flat boxes
- 10 AV boxes
- 1 travel trunk

**About Dennis McNally**: 
Dennis McNally (1949-) is an author, historian, and the Grateful Dead's former publicist. He became the band's authorized historian in 1980 and was hired as their publicist in 1984. McNally toured with the Grateful Dead from 1984 to 1995. In 2002, he published "A Long Strange Trip: The Inside Story of the Grateful Dead." McNally continued his duties as historian and publicist for Grateful Dead Productions until it dissolved in 2004.

**Collection Contents**:
The Dennis McNally papers comprise writings, notes, drafts, correspondence, and ephemera compiled by McNally during his time working as a historian and publicist for the Grateful Dead.

**Series Organization**:

**Series 1: Historian**
- Subseries 1.1: A Long Strange Trip: The Inside History of the Grateful Dead (extensive research files and drafts)
- Subseries 1.2: Grateful Dead Tours (tickets, backstage passes, McNally's notes)
- Subseries 1.3: Realia
- Subseries 1.4: Al Aronowitz (correspondence with rock journalist)
- Subseries 1.5: Lyrics and Music (Grateful Dead music and lyrics)

**Series 2: Publicist**
- Correspondence
- Tour documents
- Meeting minutes
- Press materials

**Series 3: Photographs, Slides, and Negatives**
- Promotional images
- Tour photographs
- Candid images of the band

**Acquisition**: Gift of Dennis McNally, 2010-2012
**Status**: Collection is open for research


### Grateful Dead Records: Decorated Envelopes (MS 332 Ser. 11), 1983-1996

**Extent**: 30 Linear Feet (28 boxes)

**Description**: 
This series includes decorated No.10 business envelopes sent to the Grateful Dead Ticket Service by fans when requesting tickets. Mickey Hart called these "masterpieces" showing the "love" between the band and the fans.

**Date Range**: 1983-1996

**Arrangement**: 
With the exception of two short runs in Box 1 and the oversize envelopes in Box 28, the envelopes are arranged chronologically.

**Acquisition**: 
Gift of Grateful Dead Productions, 2008. First accrual received in 2008, second accrual received in June 2012.

**Physical Location**: 
Stored offsite at NRLF (Northern Regional Library Facility); advance notice is required for access.

**Digital Access**: 
Selected images may be viewed at the Grateful Dead Archive Online (GDAO)

**Status**: Collection is open for research

**Significance**: 
This collection represents the unique bond between the Grateful Dead and their fans (Deadheads). Fans decorated envelopes with elaborate artwork, drawings, collages, and creative designs when requesting concert tickets through the mail-order ticket service. These envelopes became works of art in themselves and demonstrate the creative engagement of the fan community.


## Dead Central Gallery Space

**Location**: Main Floor of McHenry Library at UC Santa Cruz

**Size**: 1,400 square feet (repurposed classroom with glass walls)

**Purpose**: A gallery space dedicated to exploring cultural, social, and creative moments in the twentieth century in which the Grateful Dead played a critical part.

**Funding**: Creation made possible through a generous grant from the Brittingham Family Foundation

**Access**: Free and open to the public during all McHenry Library hours

**Features**: Exhibits feature unique materials from the Grateful Dead Archive alongside original sources from across the University Library's rich historical collections

### Current Exhibition (2025)

**"All the Pages Are My Days": Print and Visual Culture of the Grateful Dead in Context**
- Opened: June 2025
- Focus: Art and print material generated by the Grateful Dead and their fans in the context of the printing explosion of the 1960s and '70s
- Themes: Democratic print technologies, underground press, psychedelic art, alternative publications, counterculture movements
- Materials: Posters, pamphlets, handbills, flyers, spirit duplicators, mimeographs, experimental poetry, political action materials

### Previous Exhibitions

1. **"The Thread that Runs so True: Archival Connections of the Grateful Dead"** (2024)
   - Explored connections between the Grateful Dead and other artists, thinkers, and supporters whose archives are at UCSC
   - Featured materials from multiple UCSC collections including avant garde composers, jazz and rock innovators, Black Panther Party members, sound studies, and gonzo journalism

2. **"When We Paint Our Masterpiece: The Art of the Grateful Dead Community"** (February 2, 2020 - December 22, 2022)
   - Featured artwork sent to the band from around the world by fans
   - Included pencil sketches, abstract works, portraits, screenprints, sculptures, comic art, Arts & Crafts-inspired drawings
   - Documented the transnational Dead Head community, including countries where the band never toured (like Japan)
   - Virtual 360-degree tour available online

3. **"Love on Haight: the Grateful Dead and San Francisco in 1967"** (June 6, 2017 - May 1, 2018)
   - Explored the Summer of Love through posters, photography, and ephemera
   - Featured documents from the Grateful Dead Archive, Ruth-Marion Baruch's 1967 Haight-Ashbury photographs
   - Included alternative publications: newspapers, magazines, comic books, literary journals, broadsides, political tracts
   - Digital exhibit available online

4. **"Songs of Our Own: The Art of the Grateful Dead Phenomenon"** (April 27, 2013 - April 30, 2014)
   - Explored the art that documented, celebrated, and inspired the Grateful Dead and their fans

### Virtual Tours

The archive offers virtual, 360-degree tours of past Dead Central exhibits, allowing remote access to exhibition materials.
