# UC Santa Cruz Grateful Dead Archive: Comprehensive Summary of Holdings

## Overview

The Grateful Dead Archive at the University of California, Santa Cruz represents one of the most significant popular culture collections of the 20th century. The archive documents the band's activity and influence in contemporary music from 1965 to 1995, with some materials dating before and after that period.

## History and Donation

### April 24, 2008: Official Donation

On April 24, 2008, band members Bob Weir and Mickey Hart announced at the San Francisco Fillmore press conference that the Grateful Dead was donating its archives to UC Santa Cruz Library Special Collections.

**Why Santa Cruz?**

Bob Weir explained: "We looked around, and UC Santa Cruz seems the best possible home. If you ever wrote the Grateful Dead a letter, you'll probably find it there!"

The band had a pre-existing connection to UCSC through Mickey Hart's collaboration with the late UCSC Professor Fredric Lieberman on three books, including *Planet Drum*. Dr. Lieberman taught the course "Music of the Grateful Dead," where students discovered how the band re-envisioned and updated many of America's musical traditions.

### Eileen Law: Original Archivist

Eileen Law, originally hired to serve as a liaison to fans, became the band's original archivist. For over thirty-four years she saved everything. As she described it: "It started off in my little closet" at the Dead's headquarters in San Rafael, "and it kept growing and growing, and now it fills up a warehouse."

## Main Grateful Dead Records Collections

The band's archive is organized into twelve major series:

### Series 1: History
Documents the band's formation, development, and historical milestones.

### Series 2: Business
Business records documenting tours, concerts, and commercial operations.

### Series 3: Show Files (1965-2005)
Materials documenting concerts performed by the Grateful Dead, as well as some show materials from solo and side projects with band members.

### Series 4: Press
Press materials, clippings, and media coverage.

### Series 5: Correspondence
Letters and correspondence between the band, fans, business associates, and others.

### Series 6: Photographs
Extensive photographic documentation including promotional, tour, and candid images.

### Series 7: Media
Audio and video recordings (note: the band still controls its legendary tape Vault; Special Collections holds copies of commercially released recordings).

### Series 8: Posters
The poster collection is one of the most commonly requested parts of the archive. In the 1960s, the Grateful Dead played a defining role in what has come to be called the San Francisco Poster Renaissance. The collection contains works by some of the most famous artists of the era.

### Series 9: Artwork
Visual materials created by members of the Grateful Dead and others, including sketches, illustrations, and paintings. Features works by renowned artists including:
- Herb Greene
- Stanley Mouse
- Wes Wilson
- Susana Millman

### Series 10: Realia
Three-dimensional objects and artifacts, including equipment and memorabilia.

### Series 11: Decorated Envelopes (1983-1996)
**Extent:** 30 linear feet (28 boxes)

This remarkable collection includes decorated No. 10 business envelopes sent to the Grateful Dead Ticket Service by fans when requesting tickets. Mickey Hart called these "masterpieces" showing the "love" between the band and the fans. The envelopes feature elaborate artwork, drawings, collages, and creative designs, representing the unique bond between the band and Deadheads.

**Arrangement:** Chronological (with some exceptions)
**Physical Location:** Stored offsite at NRLF (Northern Regional Library Facility); advance notice required
**Digital Access:** Selected images viewable on GDAO

### Series 12: Back Stage Passes, Tickets and Laminates
Documentation of the band's touring and access credentials.

## Special Strengths of the Collection

The archive's particular strengths include:

1. **Business Records:** Comprehensive documentation of tours and concerts spanning three decades

2. **Visual Materials:** Posters, artwork, and merchandise documenting the Grateful Dead's broader commercial efforts and visual identity

3. **Fan Materials:** Thousands of decorated envelopes, fan art, and correspondence revealing the extraordinary bond the band forged with their fans (the Deadheads)

4. **Publications:** 
   - Full runs of Dead Heads newsletters
   - Complete collection of the Grateful Dead Almanac

5. **Artwork and Design:** Works by iconic artists of the psychedelic era who defined the band's visual aesthetic

## Grateful Dead Archive Online (GDAO)

### About GDAO

The Grateful Dead Archive Online is a socially constructed collection comprised of over 45,000 digitized items drawn from the UCSC Library's extensive Grateful Dead Archive and from digital content submitted by the community and global network of Grateful Dead fans.

### Development and Funding

**2009 IMLS Grant:** UCSC received an Institute for Museum and Library Services grant to digitize and build the GDAO socially constructed collection online.

### Technology and Partners

- **Platform:** Powered by open-source web-publishing platform Omeka
- **Features:** Supports display of collections and exhibits, social media tools, user contributions
- **Internet Archive:** Brewster Kahle supported work to integrate fan tapes preserved by the Internet Archive
- **California Digital Library:** Provided tools including:
  - EZID (persistent identifiers)
  - WAS (Web Archiving Service)
  - Merritt repository (digital preservation)
- **Design:** Santa Cruz company Cosmic: Big Ideas designed the look and feel

### Types of Digitized Content

- Concert hotline recordings
- Decorated fan envelopes
- Fanzines
- Photographs
- Posters
- Radio interviews
- Tickets
- T-shirts
- Videos
- Web resources (including David Dodd's "The Annotated Grateful Dead Lyrics" website)
- Fan recordings of concerts archived by the Internet Archive

### Community Participation

Based on the unique relationship between the band and their fans and the tape-sharing traditions, GDAO was built as a socially constructed collection. Users can:
- Tag and comment on items
- Upload and share their digital files
- Contribute memories and knowledge
- Participate in building this educational resource

## Dead Central Gallery Space

### About Dead Central

**Location:** Main Floor of McHenry Library at UC Santa Cruz
**Size:** 1,400 square feet (repurposed classroom with glass walls)
**Access:** Free and open to the public during all McHenry Library hours
**Funding:** Made possible through a generous grant from the Brittingham Family Foundation

**Mission:** Dead Central is dedicated to exploring cultural, social, and creative moments in the twentieth century in which the Grateful Dead played a critical part. Exhibits feature unique materials from the Grateful Dead Archive alongside original sources from across the University Library's rich historical collections.

### Current Exhibition (2025)

**"All the Pages Are My Days": Print and Visual Culture of the Grateful Dead in Context**
- **Opened:** June 2025
- **Focus:** Art and print material generated by the Grateful Dead and their fans in the context of the printing explosion of the 1960s and '70s
- **Themes:** 
  - Democratic print technologies
  - Underground press and alternative publications
  - Psychedelic art and experimental design
  - Counterculture movements and political activism
  - Spirit duplicators, mimeographs, and accessible printing technologies
- **Context:** Places the Dead's print culture within the broader social and political movements of the era, including civil rights, worldwide independence movements, and the "New Left"

### Previous Exhibitions

1. **"The Thread that Runs so True: Archival Connections of the Grateful Dead" (2024)**
   - Explored connections between the Grateful Dead and other artists, thinkers, and supporters
   - Drew on multiple UCSC archival collections
   - Featured materials from avant garde composers, jazz and rock innovators, Black Panther Party members, sound studies scholars, and gonzo journalism

2. **"When We Paint Our Masterpiece: The Art of the Grateful Dead Community" (February 2, 2020 - December 22, 2022)**
   - Featured artwork sent to the band from around the world by fans
   - Included diverse media: pencil sketches, abstract works, portraits, screenprints, sculptures, comic art, Arts & Crafts-inspired drawings
   - Documented the transnational Dead Head community, including countries where the band never toured (like Japan)
   - Demonstrated the possibilities of fan culture and collaborative creativity
   - Virtual 360-degree tour available online

3. **"Love on Haight: the Grateful Dead and San Francisco in 1967" (June 6, 2017 - May 1, 2018)**
   - Explored the Summer of Love through posters, photography, and ephemera
   - Featured documents from the Grateful Dead Archive
   - Included Ruth-Marion Baruch's 1967 Haight-Ashbury photographs
   - Alternative publications: newspapers, magazines, comic books, literary journals, broadsides, political tracts
   - Audiovisual components: films, performance snippets, music
   - Digital exhibit available online

4. **"Songs of Our Own: The Art of the Grateful Dead Phenomenon" (April 27, 2013 - April 30, 2014)**
   - Explored the art that documented, celebrated, and inspired the Grateful Dead and their fans

### Virtual Tours

The archive offers virtual, 360-degree tours of past Dead Central exhibits, allowing remote access to exhibition materials for those unable to visit in person.

## Notable Aspects and Significance

### Cultural Significance

The Grateful Dead Archive represents:

1. **A Unique Band-Fan Relationship:** The archive documents the extraordinary bond between the band and their fans, including the decorated envelopes that Mickey Hart called "masterpieces"

2. **Counterculture Documentation:** Materials documenting the 1960s counterculture, psychedelic movement, and social change

3. **Musical Innovation:** Records of the band's improvisational approach and re-envisioning of American musical traditions (bluegrass, folk, jazz, rock)

4. **Visual Culture:** The San Francisco Poster Renaissance and psychedelic art movement

5. **Business Model Innovation:** Documentation of the band's unique approach to the music business, including their pioneering tape-trading policy

6. **Community Building:** Evidence of a transnational fan community that persists decades after the band's dissolution

### Archival Innovation

The Grateful Dead Archive is notable for:

1. **Socially Constructed Collection:** GDAO's model of combining institutional holdings with community-contributed content

2. **Digital Access:** Over 45,000 items digitized and freely available online

3. **Fan Participation:** Encouraging fans to contribute their materials, knowledge, and memories

4. **Interdisciplinary Research:** Supporting research across music, cultural studies, business, art history, social movements, and more

## Copyright and Use

### Copyright Information

Items from the Grateful Dead Archive have been added to GDAO when:
- Permission has been received
- Works are determined to be in the public domain
- Use is believed to be fair use as part of a non-commercial, educational site

## Summary

The UC Santa Cruz Grateful Dead Archive is a comprehensive, world-class collection documenting one of America's most influential and beloved rock bands. With nearly 1,000 boxes of materials, over 45,000 digitized items, and numerous related collections, the archive provides unparalleled resources for understanding the Grateful Dead phenomenon, 1960s counterculture, American music history, and the unique relationship between artists and their community.

The archive continues to grow through donations, community contributions, and ongoing digitization efforts, ensuring that the Grateful Dead's legacy remains accessible to researchers, fans, and future generations.
