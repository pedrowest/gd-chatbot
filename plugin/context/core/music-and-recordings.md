## Musical Catalog & Performance

### Song Catalog
- **Total Songs:** 300+ (originals and covers)
- **Most-Played Song:** "Playing in the Band" (~580 performances)
- **Original Compositions:** Written by Garcia/Hunter, Weir/Barlow, and others
- **Cover Songs:** Extensive catalog from blues, folk, rock, and traditional sources

### Setlist Patterns

**Common Openers:**
- "Jack Straw"
- "Bertha"
- "Mississippi Half-Step Uptown Toodeloo"
- "The Music Never Stopped"
- "Hell in a Bucket" (1980s-90s)

**Common Closers:**
- "Not Fade Away" > "Goin' Down the Road Feeling Bad"
- "One More Saturday Night" (Saturdays)
- "Johnny B. Goode"

**Encore Songs:**
- "U.S. Blues"
- "Casey Jones"
- "Brokedown Palace"
- "Black Muddy River" (later years)

**Song Evolution:**
- Arrangements changed significantly across eras
- Tempo variations (e.g., "Playing in the Band" slower in 70s, faster in 80s)
- Key changes and structural modifications
- Some songs retired, others revived after years

### Improvisational Structures

**Jam Vehicles:**
- "Dark Star" - The ultimate vehicle for extended improvisation
- "Playing in the Band" - Often featured extended outro jams
- "The Other One" - High-energy improvisation
- "Terrapin Station" - Suite-like structured improvisation
- "Eyes of the World" - Jazzy, melodic jamming

**Space/Drums:**
- "Drums" - Percussion duet (Bill & Mickey)
- "Space" - Ambient, experimental segment
- Standard second-set structure: Song > Drums > Space > Song
- Electronic experimentation, feedback manipulation
- MIDI integration in 1980s

**Segues & Transitions:**
- Seamless song-to-song transitions
- Common pairings: "Scarlet Begonias" > "Fire on the Mountain"
- Medleys and suites
- Spontaneous key modulations

### Lyrical Themes (Robert Hunter)
- Americana and folk traditions
- Cosmic/mystical imagery
- Outlaw narratives
- Death and mortality
- Love and loss
- Journey and transformation
- Literary and mythological references

### Archive & Taping Culture

**Internet Archive (archive.org/details/GratefulDead)**
- Most comprehensive collection of live recordings
- Thousands of shows available for streaming and download
- Audience and soundboard recordings
- Free access, preserving taping culture

**Recording Types:**
- **Soundboard (SBD):** Direct from mixing board, highest quality
- **Audience (AUD):** Recorded in venue, captures atmosphere
- **Matrix:** Blend of soundboard and audience mics (Note: "Matrix" as a recording type is different from "The Matrix" venue in San Francisco)

**Taper Section:**
- Designated area for recording equipment
- Band's permission revolutionized concert recording
- Strict rules: no selling, only trading
- Equipment evolution: cassette → DAT → digital

**Notable Tapers:**
- Charlie Miller - Renowned for high-quality recordings and remastering
- Betty Cantor-Jackson - Official recordings, "Betty Boards"
- Various community members whose recordings became legendary
