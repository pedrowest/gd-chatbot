## Equipment & Gear

### Jerry Garcia's Guitars

#### Wolf (1973-1979)
- **Builder:** Doug Irwin
- **Features:** Custom single-cutaway, mahogany body, maple neck
- **Pickup:** Irwin-designed single coil
- **Notable:** Named for the wolf sticker below bridge
- **Sound:** Warm, clear tone with sustain
- **Famous Performances:** Mid-to-late 1970s prime period

#### Tiger (1979-1990)
- **Builder:** Doug Irwin
- **Features:** Cocobolo wood body, complex electronics
- **Innovation:** MIDI implementation in 1980s
- **Appearance:** Distinctive tiger-stripe inlay
- **Sound:** Rich, versatile tones
- **Era:** Peak commercial period, MIDI experimentation

#### Rosebud (1990-1993)
- **Builder:** Doug Irwin
- **Features:** Figured maple top
- **Design:** Similar to Tiger with refined aesthetics
- **Period:** Early 1990s shows

#### Lightning Bolt (1993-1995)
- **Builder:** Stephen Cripe
- **Features:** Bolt of lightning inlay down fretboard
- **Significance:** Garcia's final main guitar
- **Sound:** Bright, articulate tone
- **Legacy:** Used during final tours

#### Earlier Guitars
- **Guild Starfire** - Early years
- **Gibson SG** - Mid-1960s
- **Alligator** (1971-1973) - Stratocaster with custom work, alligator sticker
- Various Fender Stratocasters

### Sound System Evolution

#### Wall of Sound (1974)
- **Designers:** Owsley "Bear" Stanley, Dan Healy
- **Concept:** Each instrument had its own dedicated speaker system
- **Size:** Massive PA, 75 feet wide, 6 stories tall
- **Innovation:** Unprecedented clarity and volume
- **Challenge:** Extremely difficult to tour, required multiple trucks
- **Impact:** Revolutionary but ultimately impractical
- **End:** Retired after 1974 hiatus

#### Post-Wall Systems
- **Meyer Sound** systems (1975-1995)
- Ultrasonic monitoring innovations
- Continuous refinement of live sound quality
- Dan Healy's ongoing innovations
