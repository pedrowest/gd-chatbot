# Jerry Garcia Equipment

## Guitars

### Alligator (1955 Fender Stratocaster)
- Played: 1971-1973
- Purchased from Phoenix pawnshop for $250 in late 1970
- Gift from Graham Nash
- 1955 swamp-ash body and 1955 maple neck
- Modified frequently by Frank Fuller and Rick Turner of Alembic
- Identified by alligator sticker on pickguard
- Last played: Roosevelt Stadium 8/1/73
- Sold at auction in 2019 for $420,000

### Travis Bean Guitars
- TB500 models and TB1000A models
- Played: 1975-1978
- Solid Hawaiian Koa bodies with aluminum necks
- Schaller tuning keys, brass nut, 22 frets, rosewood fingerboard
- TB500 #12 played at Barton Hall 5/8/77
- First guitars to feature "Synth" pickup and OBEL FX system

### Wolf (Doug Irwin Custom)
- Played: 1973-1993
- Commissioned for $1,500 in 1972
- Made from purpleheart and curly maple with ebony fingerboard
- 24 frets
- Eagle logo and wolf inlay
- First played: 9/5/73 at Pirates Ball NYC
- Last played: 2/23/93 Oakland Coliseum
- Sold in 2017 for $1.6 million (matched to $3.2 million total for SPLC)

### Tiger (Doug Irwin Custom)
- Played: 1979-1995
- Commissioned for $5,800
- "Hippie sandwich" construction with cocobolo, vermilion, maple
- Solid brass binding and hardware
- Weight: 13.5 lbs
- Tiger inlay below tailpiece
- 12 discrete voices/tones
- First played: 8/4/79 Oakland Auditorium
- Last played: 7/9/95 Soldier Field Chicago
- Sold in 2002 for $850,000 to Jim Irsay

### Rosebud (Doug Irwin Custom)
- Played: 1990-1995
- Commissioned for $11,000
- Delivered late 1989
- MIDI controls built in
- Hollowed-out flame-maple core
- Weight: 11.5 lbs
- Dancing skeleton inlay ("The Saint")
- First played: 12/31/89 Oakland Coliseum
- Last played: 7/9/95 Soldier Field Chicago
- Currently at Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

### Lightning Bolt (Stephen Cripe Custom)
- Played: 1993-1995
- Gift from Stephen Cripe
- Recycled Brazilian rosewood fingerboard
- East Indian rosewood body
- Lightning bolt inlay
- MIDI-compatible (modified by Gary Brawer)
- First played: 8/7/93 Seattle Memorial Stadium
- Last played: 6/4/95 Shoreline Amphitheater
- Currently at Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

### Top Hat (Stephen Cripe Custom)
- Delivered April 1995
- Commissioned for $6,500
- Walnut core with laminated cocobolo top and back
- Maple and rosewood neck
- Recycled ivory inlays
- Warthog tusk inlay (top hat design)
- Black Schaller hardware
- Rarely played
- Currently at Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

## Amplifiers
- Fender Twin Reverb (primary amp)
- Used in stock form early on, later modified

## Effects
- OBEL FX system (custom effects loop)
- Vox Crybaby wah
- Boss HM-2 Heavy Metal distortion pedal
- Various custom buffers and signal processing

## EQ Settings (typical)
- Treble: 10
- Middle: 5.5
- Bass: off
- Bright switch: down position
